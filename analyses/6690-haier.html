Midea at 12×. Electrolux at 14×. The World's Largest Appliance Maker Trades at 6.85×.

Haier Smart Home (6690.HK) owns GE Appliances in the United States, Fisher & Paykel in Australia, and the number one appliance brand in China. A weak final quarter and a tariff announcement sent the stock sharply lower. The company did not change. The multiple did.

Three events arrived in rapid succession for Haier Smart Home (6690.HK). In March 2026, the FY2025 annual results revealed a Q4 operating profit that fell −39% year-on-year to CNY 2.2 billion, with China domestic sales down −15% in the quarter. In April, the United States announced sweeping tariffs on Chinese goods, raising the effective rate to 31.6%. Analyst price targets fell across the board. The stock, which had traded at HKD 28.20, sat at HKD 20.76 on April 25, 2026, a single point above its 52-week low.

The business runs ROIC of 19.3%. Its enterprise value divided by operating profit is 6.85×.

Midea trades at 12×. Electrolux at 14×. Haier shipped more appliances than either of them last year. That is the arithmetic.

What Haier Does

This is not a China brand with overseas aspirations. Haier Smart Home operates five distinct brands across 160 countries with independent manufacturing bases on multiple continents. In China, the Haier brand holds the #1 market share in white goods and runs the Casarte premium line. In the United States, GE Appliances manufactures refrigerators in Louisville, Kentucky, and dishwashers in Kinston, North Carolina. American factories, American workers. In Australia and New Zealand, Fisher & Paykel operates its own engineering and production facilities. In Europe, Candy and Hoover complete the global footprint.

FY2025 revenue across all five brands was CNY 302.3 billion, up +5.7% year-on-year, sourced from the HKEX annual results filing dated March 27, 2026. Free cash flow was CNY 17.2 billion. The balance sheet carries net cash of CNY 2.3 billion. ROIC of 19.3% (measured on equity plus net debt) has held for three consecutive fiscal years. Interest is covered 9.76× by operating earnings. An active buyback of CNY 4.5 billion is running on the A-share market.

Gross margin was 26.1%.

That last number is the one the market has decided to price.

Why the Discount Exists

Three compression forces converged in the same window. The market has read all three as permanent.

The first was the quarterly result. The full-year FY2025 numbers looked defensible: revenue +5.7%, net profit +4.4%. The Q4 decomposition was not. Operating profit in the final quarter fell −39% to CNY 2.2 billion. Revenue fell −7%. China domestic sales, the group's highest-margin market, fell −15% in the quarter. The annual result masked a deteriorating exit rate.

The second was gross margin. From 30.6% in FY2022, the consolidated gross margin has compressed to 26.1% in FY2025, a decline of 4.5 percentage points over three years. The cause has not been disclosed by segment. Haier does not publish domestic versus overseas gross margin in its results communiqué. Without that breakdown, the market cannot distinguish cyclical compression (domestic price war, raw materials) from structural dilution (overseas businesses running below 20% gross margin and growing as a share of the mix). It is pricing the worst interpretation.

The third was Liberation Day. On April 2, 2026, the United States announced tariffs that raised the effective rate on Chinese goods to 31.6%. GE Appliances manufactures in the United States, but its component supply chain runs partly through China: compressors, electronics, mechanical parts. The magnitude of the tariff impact on GEA margins has not been disclosed. Jefferies reduced their price target to HKD 23 from HKD 28 and maintained their Hold. CLSA moved to HKD 25. Both revised targets remain above HKD 20.76.

Three forces compressed the price. None of them changed the cash flow.

Three Facts the Current Price Has Not Processed

The market has priced all three compression forces as permanent. Two of them are not.

  1. 01 The GEA tariff risk is unquantified, not confirmed. GE Appliances builds American products in American factories. The tariff exposure sits on imported components, a fraction of the bill of materials, not the revenue line. Management has issued no profit warning. Jefferies and CLSA, the two banks that cut their targets most visibly, both retained their positive ratings and see double-digit upside from HKD 20.76. The tariff risk is real. It is not the −26% decline implies.
  2. 02 The H-share discount to the A-share has reached a historical extreme. Haier's H-shares (6690.HK) trade at approximately 30% below the identical A-shares on the Shanghai exchange (600690.SS). That spread widens precisely when mainland Southbound flows slow, which is the environment April 2026 represents. Every prior cycle of this spread expansion has corrected as flows normalised. A mechanical compression of the H/A discount alone, without any change in the underlying business, returns the H-share to HKD 27–29.
  3. 03 The one genuine uncertainty resolves in August 2026. The H1 FY2026 interim filing will disclose a six-month margin profile that includes the full tariff regime and reflects the current demand environment. If H1 gross margin holds at 26.5% or above, the three-year compression story is cyclical and the re-rating thesis stands. If it falls below 25%, the structural case gains evidence. One data point settles what the current price cannot distinguish. That data point is eight weeks away.

What the Company Is Worth

One method. EV/EBIT.

EBIT for FY2025 is CNY 26.158 billion, sourced from the HKEX annual results filing dated March 27, 2026. Enterprise value at HKD 20.76 is approximately HKD 192.2 billion, after netting CNY 2.3 billion in net cash at the FX rate of 1.073. EV/EBIT is 6.85×.

The peer universe is documented: Midea at 12×, Gree at 8×, Whirlpool at 10×, Electrolux at 14×, Samsung Electronics at 11×. Peer average: 11×. Haier is the volume leader among them and trades at a 38% discount to the group.

Risks We Are Not Downplaying

Two risks are documented. Neither is hypothetical.

Risk 1: Gross margin compression may be structural

The 4.5-percentage-point decline from FY2022 to FY2025 could be a permanent feature of Haier's mix shift. If GE Appliances, Fisher & Paykel, Candy, and Hoover collectively run gross margins structurally below 20%, and their share of consolidated revenue continues to grow, the compression continues regardless of what happens in China's domestic cycle. The re-rating thesis requires that 26% is the floor, not the trajectory. If H1 FY2026 gross margin prints below 25%, the structural case gains decisive evidence and the path to 11× EV/EBIT closes. The segment data to resolve this is not disclosed in the results communiqué. It sits in the full annual report PDF and in the H1 filing. Until one of those appears, this risk remains open.

Risk 2: GEA tariff exposure is unquantified

GEA's supply chain uses Chinese components for compressors, electronics, and mechanical assemblies. At an effective tariff rate of 31.6% on Chinese-origin inputs, a meaningful exposure could compress GEA's margins permanently. Management has not disclosed the magnitude. If the annualised impact exceeds CNY 2 billion (plausible if component exposure represents 15–20% of GEA cost of goods), consolidated EBIT falls by approximately 8%, pushing EV/EBIT from 6.85× to approximately 7.4×. The arithmetic still holds, but the buffer to the exit zone narrows. The H1 FY2026 earnings call, expected August 2026, is the first public forum where management is likely to quantify this.

A third risk sits beneath both: if China domestic appliance demand deteriorates further in FY2026 (property market depressed, trade-in subsidy not renewed, household confidence suppressed), the recovery timeline for the highest-margin segment extends. Duration for this risk is indeterminate from public data.

The Decision

Written at HK$20.76 on April 25, 2026. The price level is part of the thesis.

This is a valuation trade. The thesis does not require Haier to accelerate its growth. It requires the market to stop pricing the world's largest appliance manufacturer by volume at a 38% discount to peers that produce less revenue with lower market share.

From HKD 20.76, the base case at HKD 33.70 is +62%. The exit zone (if FY2026 gross margin falls below 24% and revenue growth turns negative simultaneously) is HKD 18–19, a downside of −9 to −13%. The asymmetry at current levels is approximately 5:1 in favour of the long position. The dividend yield of 6.4% reduces the cost of waiting for the re-rating.
